Inclusive Pedagogy for Migrant Children

Inclusive Content

Multicultural Curriculum

Include histories, literature, arts, and perspectives from the diverse cultural backgrounds of migrant students.

Teach about the cultures, traditions, and experiences of different migrant communities.

Incorporate multilingual resources and encourage students to share their linguistic abilities.

Career and Life Skills

Integrate career exploration and workforce readiness activities tailored to the diverse aspirations and backgrounds of migrant students.

Teach practical life skills, such as financial literacy, digital literacy, and navigating local systems and institutions.

Collaborate with local organizations to offer internships, apprenticeships, and job placement support for migrant youth.

Social-Emotional Learning

Provide support for the unique social-emotional needs of migrant children, such as managing trauma, building resilience, and navigating cultural adjustments.

Inclusive Activities

Sharing New Regarding Hometowns

Enhance the migrant children's connection with hometown.

Creating Class Hometown Maps

Reinforce the sense of belonging of migrant children.

Accent Appreciation

Educate all students, staff, and families about the value of linguistic diversity.

Dispel myths and misconceptions about accents being "right" or "wrong."

Encourage students to share their linguistic backgrounds and accent stories.
